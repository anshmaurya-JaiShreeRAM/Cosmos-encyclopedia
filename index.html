🛸 INTERNATIONAL SPACE STATION

The International Space Station is a modular space station in low Earth orbit. It is a multinational collaborative project involving five space agencies.

Orbiting at an altitude of approximately 408 kilometers the ISS travels at 28000 kilometers per hour completing 16 orbits per day.

Continuously inhabited since November 2000

📖 SPACE DICTIONARY

Key Terms of Space Science Explained Simply

BLACK HOLE

A region in space where gravity is so strong that nothing not even light can escape from it

LIGHT YEAR

The distance that light travels in one year approximately 9.46 trillion kilometers

NEBULA

A giant cloud of gas and dust in space where new stars are born

ORBIT

The curved path that an object takes around another object due to gravity

SUPERNOVA

A powerful explosion of a massive star at the end of its life

DARK MATTER

Invisible matter that makes up 27 percent of the total universe

EXOPLANET

A planet that orbits a star outside our own solar system

SOLAR WIND

Stream of charged particles released from the upper atmosphere of the Sun
